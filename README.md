# BovWGS-Pipeline
## Whole-genome sequences of 240 indigenous African cattle from Egypt, Uganda, and South Africa
Indigenous cattle are central to livestock production in Africa, valued for their adaptability to harsh tropical environments despite lower productivity compared to commercial breeds. Genome analyses offer critical insights into the genetic potential for enhancing both resilience and productive traits, supporting the advancement of worldwide cattle farming systems.
Here, we generated whole-genome sequence data for 240 indigenous cattle representing breeds from distinct agro-climatic regions in Egypt, Uganda, and South Africa. The dataset comprises over ten terabytes of paired-end reads generated using the Illumina NovaSeq 6000 platform, with an average genome coverage of approximately 10×. Post-filtering sequencing reads were mapped to the ARS-UCD1.2 reference genome with a mean mapping rate of 99.2% (range: 64.5–99.9%).Variant calling identified approximately 44 million SNPs and 3.8 million indels (length ≤ 50 bp) distributed unevenly across the genome. Functional annotation linked many of these variants to known genes and regulatory elements. This comprehensive genomic resource provides a valuable foundation for future studies of genetic diversity, breed identity, population structure, local adaptation, breed-specific traits, and strategies for global cattle conservation.

## Data availability
Raw sequencing data are available in the ENA under Project accession PRJEB90914. VCFs, including both the primary unfiltered dataset and the filtered dataset used for downstream analyses, are available in the EVA under Project accessions PRJEB94226 (ARS-UCD1.2) and PRJEB93868 (ARS-UCD2.0 Y chromosome).
